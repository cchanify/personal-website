Baseball dice

Drew Friedrichs and Connor Hanify

Take turns rollling dice to see who will win the Baseball Game!

Roll outcomes:

3 – triple: batter goes to third base and base runners advance three bases.

4 – double: batter goes to second and base runners advance two bases.

5 – walk: batter goes to first and base runners advance one base if necessary.

6 – single: batter goes to first and base runners advance one base.

7 – single: bateter goes to first and base runners advance one base.

8 – if “8” is rolled, things get a little complicated as multiple things could happen:

   -If first base is empty, batter reaches first and runners do not advance. 

   -If runner is on first, batter is out and first base runner is out (double-play).

   -If runners are on base, it is a passed ball: runners advance and the current batter continues.

9 – pop out: batter is out and runners do not advance.

10 – if “10” is rolled, things again get a little complicated:

  -If no one is on base, it is a ground out, and the batter is out. 

  -If runners are on base, it is a passed ball: runners advance and the current batter continues batting.

11 – strike out: batter is out and runners do not advance.

12 – pop out: batter is out and runners do not advance.

13 – pop out: batter is out and runners do not advance.

14 – fly out: batter is out, but runners do advance if less than two outs.

15 – double: batter goes to second and base runners advance two bases.

16 - double: batter goes to second and base runners advance two bases.

17- homerun: batter and all base runners score.

18 - homerun: batter and all base runners score.
